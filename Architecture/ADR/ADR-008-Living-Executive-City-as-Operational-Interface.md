# ADR-008: Living Executive City as an Operational Interface

- **Status:** Accepted
- **Date:** 2026-07-15
- **Decision owners:** Founder, Product, Engineering Lane™, Executive Council™

## Context

Traditional dashboards can display system state but do not fully express Cougar's organizational model of Foundries, Lanes, departments, agents, nodes, missions, and executive relationships.

## Decision

Cougar World will evolve into a living executive city that acts as a spatial operational interface. Districts and buildings must map to real organizational entities and eventually reflect live runtime state.

## Consequences

### Positive

- Makes a complex AI organization more understandable.
- Creates a differentiated desktop, mobile, and VR experience.
- Gives every major capability a visible organizational home.
- Enables navigable mission, department, and executive workflows.

### Tradeoffs

- Requires performance budgets and accessibility alternatives.
- 3D entities must remain synchronized with authoritative data.
- The city cannot replace all conventional administrative views.
- Visual polish must not outrun operational usefulness.

## Design rules

- No decorative district without an organizational purpose.
- Runtime data remains authoritative; the city is a projection of it.
- Every interactive entity uses stable organizational identifiers.
- Mobile and low-power fallback experiences are required.
- Security and permissions apply identically in spatial and dashboard views.
